To break down Return on Assets (ROA), we use the Strategic Profit Model. This helps us see if a companys return is driven by its profitability (Net Profit Margin) or its efficiency in using assets (Asset Turnover).

The formula is: ROA=Net Profit Margin %Asset Turnover\text{ROA} = \text{Net Profit Margin \%} \times \text{Asset Turnover}ROA=Net Profit Margin %Asset Turnover

While both companies operate in the Department Store segment, Dillard's is significantly outperforming Macy's in terms of asset productivity. Here is why:

  • Profitability Gap: Dillard's maintains a 9.2%9.2\%9.2% profit margin compared to Macy's 2.6%2.6\%2.6%. If you look at the SG&A expense percentage, Dillard's is much leaner (26.7%26.7\%26.7%) than Macy's (37.4%37.4\%37.4%), which allows more of every dollar earned to drop to the bottom line.
  • Efficiency Gap: Dillard's also turns its assets more quickly (1.81.81.8 vs 1.41.41.4). This means for every $1,000 in assets they own, they generate $1,800 in sales, whereas Macy's only generates $1,400.

does SG&A affect the net profit margin %

Yes, SG&A (Selling, General, and Administrative expenses) has a direct and significant impact on the Net Profit Margin %.

To understand why, it helps to look at where SG&A sits in the "flow" of an Income Statement:

  1. Total Revenue
  2. minus Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) = Gross Margin
  3. minus SG&A = Operating Profit
  4. minus Interest and Taxes = Net Profit

How it affects the Margin %:

Since Net Profit is what's left over after SG&A is paid, every dollar a company saves in SG&A typically goes straight to the bottom line (Net Profit).

Let's look at your current comparison to see this in action:

  • Dillard's: Has an SG&A of 26.7%. This is relatively lean for a department store. Because they keep these overhead costs low, they are able to keep 9.2% as Net Profit.
  • Macy's: Has an SG&A of 37.4%. That is a massive difference! Macy's spends over 10 cents more of every dollar earned on things like corporate salaries, advertising, and rent than Dillard's does.

Why this matters for students:

When you see a company with a high Gross Margin but a low Net Profit Margin, the "leakage" is usually happening in SG&A.

In the case of Macy's vs. Dillard's: Both companies actually have very similar Gross Margins (Dillard's: 39.5% vs. Macy's: 38.4%). This means they are both equally good at buying and selling merchandise for a profit. The reason Dillard's is so much more profitable overall is almost entirely because they are much more efficient at managing their SG&A expenses.

That is exactly the right question to ask. When two competitors have a 10.7% gap in expenses, it usually points to a fundamental difference in how they run their businesses.

While the financial data tells us what happened, we have to look at their business strategies to understand why. Here are the three main reasons Dillards keeps its SG&A so much lower than Macys:

1. Minimal Advertising Budget

If you watch TV or browse social media, you probably see Macys ads constantly. Macys spends hundreds of millions of dollars a year on national advertising, high-profile celebrity partnerships, and the famous Thanksgiving Day Parade.

  • Dillards Strategy: They spend almost nothing on national advertising. They rely on "word-of-mouth" and the fact that their stores are often the primary anchor in their local malls. This saves them a massive amount in the SG&A category.

2. Corporate "Lean-ness" and Ownership

Dillards is still heavily controlled by the Dillard family.

  • Family Oversight: Family-run businesses often have a reputation for being "frugal." They treat the company's money like their own.
  • Macy's Complexity: Macys is a massive, publicly-traded corporation with many "layers" of management, regional offices, and a heavy investment in technology and e-commerce infrastructure. All those salaries and office costs add up to higher SG&A.

3. Store Ownership vs. Leasing

This is a "hidden" factor in SG&A.

  • Ownership: Dillards owns the vast majority of their store buildings and the land they sit on. When you own the building, you don't pay rent.
  • Leasing: Macys leases a larger portion of its locations. Rent is a major component of SG&A. By owning their real estate, Dillard's eliminates a huge recurring expense that Macy's has to pay every month.

Educational Insight: The Trade-off

There is always a trade-off. By not spending on advertising or high-tech website features (SG&A), Dillard's risks losing "top of mind" awareness with younger shoppers.
